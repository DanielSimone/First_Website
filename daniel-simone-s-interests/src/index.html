3D Printing & CAD

One of the things I like to do on my custom PC is modeling up parts to 3D print with Fusion 360 (or maybe nabbing a good one off thingiverse!) I use a Prusa MK3S (pretty proud of assembling it myself!)

Many people are confused on how the printer works, so here are the five basic steps of printing:

    Five Basic Steps of 3D Printing
  1. Finding something to print (you can find files on a website like Thingiverse or design them yourself on CAD software like Fusion 360)
  2. Slicing your 3D object (this is done with software on your computer like PrusaSlicer for my printer, which slices your object into 2D "slices" for the printer to print, one on top of the other)
  3. Sending the output to your printer (the code from the slicer can be transferred to a printer by USB, SD card, or WiFi depending on your printer)
  4. Printing (your printer will heat up the plastic, extrude it, and make your object)
  5. Post-processing (you have to take the part off the printer, then sand, paint, or otherwise process it to your final result)

    P.S. If someone else with a printer sees this, check out this cool site that converts 2D objects to STL files!
